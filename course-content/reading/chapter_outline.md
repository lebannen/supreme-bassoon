## Chapter 1: La Découverte (The Discovery)

* Scene A: Sophie’s morning in her apartment. Waking up, the gray weather, feeding her cat (Minou), making coffee,
  packing her bag with specific items (pen, notebook, laptop).

* Scene B: The commute to the library. Walking in the rain, the noise of the traffic, the wet streets, people rushing
  with umbrellas.

* Scene C: Inside the library. The silence, finding the "Blue Book" in the poetry section, the envelope falling out,
  reading the mysterious note about the "Green Lion."

## Chapter 2: Le Rendez-vous (The Meeting)

* Scene A: Sophie leaves the library. She is excited but nervous. She takes her phone. She texts Marc. She describes the
  walk to a specific Café (Le Café des Arts).

* Scene B: Inside the Café. Describing the smell of coffee and croissants. Sophie waits. The waiter asks for her order.
  She orders a chocolat chaud.

* Scene C: Marc arrives. Greetings (bises). She shows him the letter. They discuss the clue. Marc thinks. He realizes
  the "Green Lion" is a fountain near the Sorbonne.

## Chapter 3: La Fontaine du Lion (The Lion Fountain)

* Scene A: Walking through the Latin Quarter. Describing the shops (bakery, bookshop) they pass. The sun comes out (
  weather change).

* Scene B: Arriving at the square. It is a small, pretty square. There is a fountain with a statue of a lion. The lion
  is green (oxidized bronze).

* Scene C: The search. They look in the water. They look behind the statue. Sophie looks under the lion's paw. She finds
  a small, waterproof box. Inside is a Metro ticket with a symbol on it.

## Chapter 4: Le Métro Mystérieux (The Mysterious Metro)

* Scene A: Going to the Metro station. Buying a ticket at the machine (step-by-step). Going down the stairs. The air is
  warm and smells distinct.

* Scene B: The platform. Waiting for the train. Watching the advertisements. The train arrives (noise). Getting on the
  train. It is crowded.

* Scene C: The ride. Looking at the map. Sophie sees a man in a Black Hat looking at them. She is scared. They get off
  at a station near the Luxembourg Gardens.

## Chapter 5: L'Ombre au Jardin (Shadow in the Garden)

* Scene A: Entering the Jardin du Luxembourg. Describing the white paths, the green chairs, and the flowers. It is
  peaceful.

* Scene B: They sit on a bench to look at the clues again. They eat a sandwich. Sophie sees the Man in the Black Hat
  again behind a tree.

* Scene C: They run to the "Statue of the Queens." They find a carving on the stone base of a statue. It points toward
  the river Seine.

## Chapter 6: Au Bord de la Seine (By the River)

* Scene A: Walking to the river. Crossing a bridge (Pont Neuf). Looking at the water. The Bateaux-Mouches (tourist
  boats) pass by.

* Scene B: The wind picks up. Sophie is holding the map/clue. The wind blows it away!

* Scene C: They chase the paper along the riverbank. A tourist stops the paper with his foot. He is nice. He gives it
  back. They see the next location written on the back: "Shakespeare & Company."

## Chapter 7: La Librairie (The Bookstore)

* Scene A: Arriving at the famous English bookstore. It is chaotic and full of books. Describing the piles of books on
  the floor and shelves.

* Scene B: They talk to the owner (an eccentric old man). He knows about Victor Hugo. He asks them a simple question
  about poetry.

* Scene C: Sophie answers correctly. The owner smiles. He gives them an old, heavy iron key. He says it opens a box in
  Victor Hugo's house.

## Chapter 8: La Confrontation (The Confrontation)

* Scene A: Walking toward the Marais district (where the house is). The streets are narrow and old.

* Scene B: They arrive at Place des Vosges. It is a square park with identical houses. The Man in the Black Hat appears
  in front of them. He blocks the path.

* Scene C: The conversation. Sophie is brave. She asks, "Who are you?" He explains: He is a librarian. He thought they
  were thieves. He wants to protect the poem. They become friends.

## Chapter 9: Le Manuscrit (The Manuscript)

* Scene A: Entering Victor Hugo’s house (now a museum). Buying tickets. Walking up the stairs.

* Scene B: The search. They go to the bedroom. They see a small wooden chest on a table.

* Scene C: Sophie uses the iron key. It fits! She turns the key. Click. Inside, there is a yellow paper. It is the lost
  poem.

## Chapter 10: Une Belle Journée (A Beautiful Day)

* Scene A: Reading the poem together. It is short and beautiful. It speaks about loving Paris.

* Scene B: They decide to give the poem to the museum curator. The curator is very happy.

* Scene C: Celebration. Sophie, Marc, and the Librarian go to a bistro. They eat dinner. Sophie realizes she understands
  the conversation. She is happy to be in Paris.
