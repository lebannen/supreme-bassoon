# French A1 Course Plan: A 10-Module Structure

This plan outlines a 10-module course for beginner French (A1 level), based on the communicative principles of the CEFR.

## Guiding Principles for the Course

* **Communicative First:** Every grammar point must unlock a specific communicative goal.
* **Spiral Curriculum:** Key concepts (like *être* and *avoir*) are introduced, then revisited and expanded upon.
* **Culture is Key:** Cultural notes (e.g., *tu* vs. *vous*, *la bise*) are woven in from day one.
* **The 4 Skills:** Each module must have components for Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing.

---

## Course Structure: French A1 (10-Module Plan)

### Module 1: The First Contact (Les Premiers Contacts)

* **Module Goal:** To say hello, goodbye, introduce oneself, and handle basic pleasantries.
* **Thematic Focus:** Greetings, introductions, and the alphabet.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * *Bonjour / Bonsoir / Salut / Au revoir*
    * *Oui / Non / Merci / S'il vous plaît*
    * *Comment ça va? / Ça va bien / mal / comme ci, comme ça*
    * The French alphabet.
    * Numbers 0-10.
* **Core Grammar:**
    * Subject Pronouns: *je, tu, il, elle, on, nous, vous, ils, elles*
    * The verbs **être** (to be) and **s'appeler** (to be called) in the present tense.
    * Basic question words: *Comment...?*
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** The concept of **tu** (informal) vs. **vous** (formal/plural).
    * **Phonetics:** Basic vowel sounds, nasal sounds (on, an, in), and the French 'R'.

---

### Module 2: You and Me (Vous et Moi)

* **Module Goal:** To ask and state personal details like age, nationality, and profession.
* **Thematic Focus:** Personal Information.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * Numbers 11-20.
    * Common nationalities (e.g., *français(e), américain(e)*).
    * Common professions (e.g., *professeur, étudiant(e)*).
* **Core Grammar:**
    * The verb **avoir** (to have) in the present tense (e.g., *J'ai 30 ans.*).
    * **Definite and Indefinite Articles:** *le, la, les* (the) & *un, une, des* (a/an, some).
    * Forming basic negative sentences: **ne... pas**.
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** Filling out a basic registration form.
    * **Phonetics:** **Liaisons** (e.g., *vous êtes*).

---

### Module 3: My World (Mon Monde)

* **Module Goal:** To describe immediate family and basic surroundings.
* **Thematic Focus:** Family & Adjectives.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * Basic family: *mère, père, frère, sœur*.
    * Basic descriptive adjectives: *grand(e), petit(e), content(e)*.
    * Basic colors.
* **Core Grammar:**
    * **Adjective Agreement:** Matching gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural).
    * Possessive Adjectives: *mon, ma, mes* (my); *ton, ta, tes* (your).
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** The concept of *la famille* in France.
    * **Phonetics:** Differentiating "u" (in *tu*) and "ou" (in *vous*).

---

### Module 4: The Daily Routine (La Routine Quotidienne)

* **Module Goal:** To talk about daily actions and tell time.
* **Thematic Focus:** Daily life, hobbies, and time.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * Telling time: *Quelle heure est-il? Il est...*
    * Days of the week.
    * Common activities: *manger, dormir, travailler, étudier*.
    * Hobbies: *aimer, adorer, détester*.
* **Core Grammar:**
    * **Regular -ER Verbs** (e.g., *parler, habiter*).
    * **Reflexive Verbs (Basics):** *se lever, se laver*.
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** The typical French day (e.g., lunch breaks, dinner times).
    * **Phonetics:** Silent final consonants (e.g., *parler*).

---

### Module 5: At the Market (Au Marché)

* **Module Goal:** To buy items at a market or shop.
* **Thematic Focus:** Food, drinks, and shopping.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * Common food items: *le pain, le fromage, les fruits, les légumes*.
    * Drinks: *l'eau, le café, le vin*.
    * Numbers 20-69.
    * *Je voudrais...* (I would like...).
    * *C'est combien?* (How much is it?).
* **Core Grammar:**
    * The **Partitive Article:** *du, de la, de l', des* (some) for food.
    * The verb **vouloir** (to want) in the polite *je voudrais* form.
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** The importance of the *boulangerie* (bakery) and the *marché* (market).
    * **Phonetics:** The "oi" sound (as in *bonsoir*).

---

### Module 6: Out on the Town (En Ville)

* **Module Goal:** To ask for and give simple directions and order at a café.
* **Thematic Focus:** The City & The Café.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * Places in town: *la gare, la banque, la pharmacie, les toilettes*.
    * Directions: *à gauche, à droite, tout droit*.
    * At the café: *un café, un croissant, l'addition*.
* **Core Grammar:**
    * The verb **aller** (to go).
    * The **Imperative (Command Form):** *Allez! Tournez!*
    * Prepositions of place: *à, dans, sur, sous, devant*.
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** Café etiquette (how to order, how to pay).
    * **Phonetics:** The "é" vs. "è" sounds (*café* vs. *mère*).

---

### Module 7: What Are We Doing? (Qu'est-ce qu'on fait?)

* **Module Goal:** To talk about abilities, preferences, and make plans.
* **Thematic Focus:** Leisure, activities, and abilities.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * Months and seasons.
    * Leisure activities: *aller au cinéma, faire du sport, lire*.
* **Core Grammar:**
    * The verbs **pouvoir** (can/to be able to) and **devoir** (must/to have to).
    * Regular **-IR verbs** (e.g., *finir* - to finish).
    * Regular **-RE verbs** (e.g., *attendre* - to wait).
    * Asking questions with **Est-ce que...**.
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** Common leisure activities in France.
    * **Phonetics:** Review of nasal sounds.

---

### Module 8: Making Plans (Faire des Projets)

* **Module Goal:** To talk about the immediate future.
* **Thematic Focus:** Plans for the weekend, weather.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * Weather: *Il fait beau, il pleut, il y a du soleil*.
    * Time markers: *demain, ce week-end, la semaine prochaine*.
* **Core Grammar:**
    * **Le Futur Proche (The Near Future):** Using *aller* + infinitive.
    * Example: *Je **vais manger** au restaurant.* (I am going to eat...).
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** How people make social plans; the importance of the weather.
    * **Phonetics:** Practice speaking in full sentences using the *futur proche*.

---

### Module 9: What Did You Do? (Qu'est-ce que tu as fait?)

* **Module Goal:** To talk about a completed action in the recent past.
* **Thematic Focus:** Recounting your day or weekend.
* **Key Vocabulary:**
    * Time markers: *hier, le week-end dernier*.
* **Core Grammar:**
    * **Le Passé Composé (The Past Tense):**
    * Part 1: Introduce *passé composé* with **avoir** only.
    * Part 2: Teach common regular past participles (*-er* -> *-é*).
    * Part 3: Teach key irregular participles: *eu* (had), *été* (been), *fait* (done), *pris* (taken).
* **Culture & Phonetics:**
    * **Culture:** The common Monday-morning question: *"Tu as fait un bon week-end?"*
    * **Phonetics:** Listening comprehension for the *passé composé*.

---

### Module 10: Putting It All Together (La Synthèse)

* **Module Goal:** To consolidate all A1 skills in a practical, real-world context.
* **Thematic Focus:** "A Day in Paris" / "My Introduction."
* **Key Vocabulary:** Review of all modules.
* **Core Grammar:** Review of Present, *Futur Proche*, and *Passé Composé* with *avoir*.
* **Final Project (Assessment):**
    * **Writing:** Write a simple postcard.
    * **Speaking:** Record a 1-minute audio/video introducing yourself.
    * **Reading:** Read a simple blog post and answer comprehension questions.
    * **Listening:** Listen to a simple weather report.
